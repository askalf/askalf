## 1. LLC vs C-Corp Comparison

| Factor | LLC | C-Corp |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Taxation** | Pass-through (no double taxation) | Double taxation (corporate + dividend) |
| **Fundraising** | Harder to raise VC (convertible notes possible) | Standard for VC investment |
| **Equity** | Membership interests (complex) | Stock (simple, standard) |
| **Formalities** | Fewer requirements | Board meetings, minutes, bylaws |
| **Flexibility** | Custom profit/loss allocation | Pro-rata to shares |
| **Exit** | More complex | Clean acquisition path |

**Recommendation:** Start as LLC if bootstrapping or seeking angel investment. Convert to C-Corp before Series A.

### ARTICLE II: MEMBERS AND CAPITAL

**2.1 Initial Members.** The initial Members, their capital contributions, and their Membership Interest percentages are set forth in Exhibit A.

**2.2 Additional Capital.** No Member shall be required to make additional capital contributions without unanimous consent.

**2.3 Capital Accounts.** A separate capital account shall be maintained for each Member.

**2.4 No Interest on Capital.** No interest shall be paid on capital contributions.

**2.5 Return of Capital.** No Member has the right to demand return of capital contributions except upon dissolution.

---

### ARTICLE III: ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**3.1 Profits and Losses.** Net profits and losses shall be allocated among Members in proportion to their Membership Interests.

**3.2 Distributions.** Distributions shall be made at such times and in such amounts as determined by the Managing Member(s), in proportion to Membership Interests.

**3.3 Tax Distributions.** The Company shall distribute to each Member an amount sufficient to cover their tax liability arising from Company income, at least annually.

---

### ARTICLE IV: MANAGEMENT

**4.1 Manager-Managed.** The Company shall be managed by one or more Managers.

**4.2 Initial Manager(s).** The initial Manager(s) shall be:
- [Founder Name] - Managing Member

**4.3 Powers of Manager.** The Manager shall have full authority to:
- Enter into contracts
- Open and manage bank accounts
- Hire employees and contractors
- Make operational decisions
- Execute documents on behalf of the Company

**4.4 Major Decisions.** The following require approval of Members holding a majority of Membership Interests:
- Sale of all or substantially all Company assets
- Merger or dissolution
- Amendment of this Agreement
- Admission of new Members
- Issuance of new Membership Interests
- Transactions exceeding $50,000
- Taking on debt exceeding $25,000
- Entering into exclusive contracts exceeding 12 months

**4.5 Compensation.** Managers may receive reasonable compensation as approved by the Members.

---

### ARTICLE V: MEMBERS' RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS

**5.1 Limited Liability.** No Member shall be personally liable for Company debts or obligations.

**5.2 No Agency.** No Member (other than a Manager) has authority to bind the Company.

**5.3 Voting.** Except as otherwise provided, Members vote in proportion to their Membership Interests.

**5.4 Meetings.** Member meetings may be called by any Manager or Members holding 25% or more of Membership Interests.

**5.5 Action Without Meeting.** Members may act by written consent signed by Members holding the required percentage.

---

### ARTICLE VI: TRANSFER OF INTERESTS

**6.1 Restrictions.** No Member may transfer their Membership Interest without:
- Written consent of Members holding a majority of Interests
- Compliance with this Article VI

**6.2 Right of First Refusal.** Before any transfer, the transferring Member must offer the Interest to existing Members at the same price and terms.

**6.3 Permitted Transfers.** Transfers are permitted without consent to:
- Revocable trusts for estate planning
- Family members (spouse, children)
- Entities wholly owned by the Member

**6.4 Drag-Along.** If Members holding 75% or more approve a sale of the Company, all Members must participate on the same terms.

**6.5 Tag-Along.** If any Member sells more than 25% of their Interest, other Members may participate pro-rata on the same terms.

---

### ARTICLE VII: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**7.1 Company IP.** All intellectual property developed by Members or employees in connection with Company business shall be owned by the Company.

**7.2 Assignment.** Each Member hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in Company IP.

**7.3 Pre-Existing IP.** Exhibit B lists any pre-existing IP contributed by Members and the terms of such contribution.

---

### ARTICLE VIII: DISSOLUTION

**8.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall dissolve upon:
- Unanimous written consent of all Members
- Judicial decree
- Any event making it unlawful to continue
- Sale of all Company assets

**8.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution:
1. Pay creditors
2. Establish reserves for contingent liabilities
3. Distribute remaining assets to Members per their Interests

---

### ARTICLE IX: MISCELLANEOUS

**9.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law.

**9.2 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members.

**9.3 Amendment.** This Agreement may only be amended by written consent of Members holding a majority of Interests.

**9.4 Severability.** If any provision is invalid, the remainder shall continue in effect.

**9.5 Dispute Resolution.** Disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Delaware.

## 7. Conversion to C-Corp

**When to convert:**
- Before Series A (VCs require C-Corp)
- When issuing stock options to employees
- When seeking Delaware C-Corp tax benefits

**Process:**
1. Draft Conversion Plan
2. Member vote (usually unanimous)
3. File Certificate of Conversion with Delaware
4. File Certificate of Incorporation
5. Exchange Membership Interests for stock
6. Issue new stock certificates

**Cost:** ~$5,000-$15,000 with attorney
